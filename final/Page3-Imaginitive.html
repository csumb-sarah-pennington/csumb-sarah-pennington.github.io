MISINFORMATION ABOUT RECYCLING

Imaginative Recycling is imagining something is recyclable when it really isn't. For instance, the thin plastic bag that comes on bread isn't recyclable yet many people recycle it because it is plastic. "People are trying to overthink the recycling and are getting very imaginative," said Kendra Kostelecky, Waste Management communication specialist.

Many don't recognize the little triangles on plastic containers or even on the recycling bins themselves. Because of this, many plastics are put in the bin when it shouldn't be there.

Even more, many recyclables become comtaminated when in the bins and therefore makes them unrecyclable. Dirty recyclables are no longer recyclable because they are meant to be remade/reconstructed.

Did you know that many plastics such as straws, bags, utensils, and various containers often cannot be recycled. These products are usually incinerated and deposited into landfils or end up in the ocean.

However, you are not specifically at fault. Our recycling system is to blame. Many of them contribte to the misconceptions and misinformation that are spread in addition to the pollutants they release.
